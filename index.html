Tiger shark

Tiger sharks are widely distributed in the tropical and subtropical zone of The world ocean, where they live both in open waters and near the coast. They are especially common near Islands in the Central Pacific ocean. They are nocturnal. These large predators reach a length of 5.5 m. They have a very diverse diet that includes crustaceans, fish, marine mammals, birds, cephalopods, sea snakes, and turtles. In the stomachs of large individuals, inedible items were found. Tiger sharks reproduce by live birth, they are fertile, and there are up to 80 newborns in the litter. These sharks are dangerous to humans. They are the object of targeted fishing. Fins, skin and liver are valued.

Hammerhead shark

The maximum recorded length is 6.1 m. These fish live in tropical and warm temperate waters of all oceans. The leading edge of the "hammer" is almost straight, which distinguishes them from other hammerhead sharks. Giant hammerhead sharks lead a solitary lifestyle, these excellent swimmers actively hunt various crustaceans, cephalopods, bony and cartilaginous fish, including sharks. Observations of sharks have led to the conclusion that with the help of a" hammer " they immobilize the tail-finders, which are their favorite prey. Giant hammerhead sharks reproduce by live birth.
